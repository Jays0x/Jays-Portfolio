Web3School E-Learning Platform

Innovative Changes On Web2 E-Learning.

Project overview

Web3School platform is an e-learning platform that utilizes blockchain technology to offer students a variety of courses, opportunities to earn rewards and interact with peers, mentors, and industry professionals. It aims to provide them with the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in today's job market.

My Responsibilities

User Research | Brainstorming | Wireframing | Visual Design

Problem Statement

The problem that E-learning platform faces is that students lack motivation, resources, and concentration when it comes to continuing their studies. This lack of motivation can stem from a variety of factors, including a lack of clear educational goals, a lack of relevant resources, and a lack of engagement with the material being studied. Additionally, students may struggle with distractions and a lack of concentration, which can impede their ability to learn and retain information. As a result, students may struggle to complete their coursework, or may not achieve the level of understanding and proficiency that they desire.

Implemented Solutions

The proposed solution for the problem of lack of motivation, resources, and concentration among students is to create a platform that incorporates features such as earning while learning, live classes, group work, assignments, empowerment program, and program planning. These features can help to increase motivation, engagement, and learning outcomes among students.

Jumping Out Of Assumption

To build usable products, you need to be out of any assumption. I need to do research on the challenges people are facing when trying to learn about web3 and how it works.

Before I do research, I asked myself the type of research methodologies to use for my research. After thinking about the approach to use, I decided to use 3 methods, user interview, behavior analysis and questionnaire To gather much insight from people.

When I've decided the type of research methods to use, I have to prepare for the question and how to approach them. I recruited 5 of my friends who are also trying to learn about web3. I sent the questionnaire to a group of developers, product managers, product designer, software engineer, and web3 enthusiast on their discord server.

As the research goes, I got 20 feedback from questionnaires and interviewed 5 of my friends. They gave me more details about what they're going through in learning web3 and how they can offer their service in web3 as a professional.
